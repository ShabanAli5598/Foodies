The Pashtun cuisine is at the heart of the food culture in Peshawar, drawing influences from eastern Afghanistan and western Pakistan. Cereals such as wheat, maize, barley and rice along with barbecued lamb, chicken, mutton, beef and fish are at the heart of many dishes. Dishes are accompanied by yoghurt and cheese-based side dishes while fresh vegetables and both dry and fresh fruit also feature strongly. Two typical dishes are kabli pulao, a rice pilaf full of lamb, cabbage, cauliflower and local nuts as well as malai tikka. This dish of skewered chicken is prepared with locally produced yoghurt and cheese and flavoured with cardamom and cumin. Any meal should be accompanied by the city's typical nans or flatbreads. Baked in typical southern Asian tandoors, these delicious loaves of bread are flavoured with cheese, garlic and mint. Diners in the city will also discover that menu inspiration is taken from Mongolian, Thai, Mughlai, Lebanese, Italian, Japanese and Mexican cuisines.
